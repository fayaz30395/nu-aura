## Executive Summary

**Total gaps identified:** 87 (after dedup across agents)
**Distribution:** 12 Critical · 27 High · 31 Medium · 17 Low

### Critical-Path Items (block go-live / production hardening)

1. **Documentation drift across foundational docs** — `CLAUDE.md` says Java 17 / next migration
   V129 / 237 pages; reality is Java 21 / V137 / 261 pages. Every architectural decision downstream
   is made on a stale baseline.
2. **Dead Letter consumer missing `PAYROLL_PROCESSING_DLT`** — Payroll DLT messages will accumulate
   with no consumer, no persistence, no alerting.
3. **Flyway `baseline-on-migrate: true` active in production** — silent baseline drift risk on first
   deploy to a populated DB; not overridden in `application-prod.yml`.
4. **Redis & Kafka have no K8s manifests** — `deployment/kubernetes/` has backend + frontend +
   postgres only. Production deploy will fail or fall back to managed services that don't exist yet.
5. **JaCoCo coverage threshold not enforced in CI** — the 80% target in `CLAUDE.md` is aspirational;
   nothing fails the pipeline below threshold.
6. **Legacy `deployment.yaml` still in repo** — labeled "DO NOT APPLY" but a
   `kubectl apply -f deployment/kubernetes/` will overwrite the correct `backend-deployment.yaml`.
7. **No CI job publishes Docker images** — `push: false` on both build-push-action steps. Deployment
   is effectively manual.
8. **No E2E gate in CI** — Playwright tests exist in `frontend/e2e/` but are not wired into
   `.github/workflows/ci.yml`.
